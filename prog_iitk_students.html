Project description

Suspension Plasma Spraying (SPS) is coating deposition technique in which a suspension with particles in sub-micrometre to nanometre range is used as a precursor to deposit coatings. This process is well suited for the deposition of materials with high melting points due to the large amount of heat generated, like ceramics. The major applications for SPS coatings include Thermal Barrier Coatings (TBCs) and Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs). In addition to being introduced to the SPS process, the project work consists of characterisation of SPS coatings fabricated for the applications mentioned above. The characterisation work will include sample preparation, mounting and polishing to analyse microstructure with techniques like optical microscopy and scanning electron microscopy. The materials present in the coating will be analysed by Electron Dispersive Spectroscopy (EDS) and X-ray Diffraction (XRD).
